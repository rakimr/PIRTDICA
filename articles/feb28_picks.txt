He is a paint-first player who gets to the rim through drives and cuts, and who collects offensive rebounds by virtue of already being in the paint when shots go up.

Washington's defense is the matchup that unlocks this. Barnes's defensive profile adds secondary value. His 2.1 blocks per game with a 2.1 per 100 rate further confirms the defensive activity level.

The book has this at -120/-106, which tells you the market sees it as close. The DVA data says it is not close. The secondary angle is rebounds over 8.5 at -102/-125.
