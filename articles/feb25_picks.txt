DE'AARON FOX: Points vs Toronto

SA @ TOR | Point Guard | Playmaker

Fox averages 19.1 points per game this season on 25.0% usage. His per 100 possessions rate of 29.9 points tells you what happens when the volume scales, and tonight it should. San Antonio is a 6.5-point road favorite in a game with a 230.5 total, the highest on the slate. Fox will play heavy minutes in a game environment designed for scoring.

His shot chart shows a diversified attacker. 21.2% of his attempts come at the restricted area, 26.6% in the paint, 14.4% from midrange, and 37.8% from three. He is not a one-zone scorer. His shot creation profile confirms it: 45.4% pull-up, 19.4% catch-and-shoot, 35.0% paint touches. He creates his own shot nearly half the time, which means his scoring does not depend on someone else setting him up.

Toronto is the matchup that makes this work. The DVA data shows Toronto gives up a +0.122 FPPM differential to Playmakers, the archetype Fox is classified as. That ranks as one of Toronto's worst defensive matchup categories. The targeted play data confirms it from the positional angle: Toronto allows +2.0 additional points of production to opposing point guards compared to league average, an 8.4% edge.

Toronto's defensive shot zone data tells the rest of the story. They allow 66.8% shooting at the restricted area and 44.3% in the paint. Their rim defense is below league average, and Fox attacks both zones with 47.8% of his total shots coming inside the paint. He also draws fouls on drives, creating free throw opportunities that inflate his scoring total beyond the field goal numbers.

The book has Fox at 16.5 points. His season average of 19.1 already clears that line by 2.6 points. The adjusted projection with DVA overlay pushes to 21.3, giving a 28.8% edge over the book line. This is the largest vs-book edge on tonight's slate for a points prop with a confirmed line.

The stat to watch: Points over 16.5 is the primary play. The 28.8% edge over book is driven by Toronto's specific vulnerability to Playmaker archetypes and their leaky interior defense. Fox's diversified shot chart means he has multiple paths to clear this line. Assists over 5.5 is the secondary angle. His 8.8 per 100 rate and Toronto's -0.122 FPPM weakness to playmaking guards create a natural scoring-plus-assists correlation.
